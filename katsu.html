Chicken Katsu

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups panko breadcrumbs
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil, for frying
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 1 large carrot, chopped
  • 1 large red bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • Rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400Â°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Pound chicken breasts to an even thickness and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Place flour in a shallow dish. In another shallow dish, beat eggs. Place breadcrumbs in a third shallow dish.
  4. Coat each chicken breast in flour, then dip in beaten eggs, and coat in breadcrumbs.
  5. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Fry chicken breasts until golden brown and cooked through, about 3-4 minutes per side. Place on the prepared baking sheet and keep warm in the oven.
  6. In the same skillet, add onion, garlic, and ginger and cook until softened, about 3 minutes. Add carrot and red bell pepper and cook until tender, about 5 minutes.
  7. Stir in flour, garam masala, curry powder, turmeric, and paprika until vegetables are coated.
  8. Stir in diced tomatoes and chicken broth. Season with salt and pepper. Bring to a simmer and let cook for 10 minutes.
  9. Stir in heavy cream and let cook for another 2 minutes.
  10. Serve chicken katsu over rice and top with sauce.
